## Quick Summary
A surface patch is a bounded piece of a smooth surface.

## Core Idea
- What it is: A surface patch is a local parametric surface segment.
- Why it matters: Patches allow complex surfaces to be built from manageable parts.
- Where it is used: It is used in CAD and spline-based modeling.

## Details
- Patches are often defined by control points and basis functions.
- Adjacent patches can be stitched with continuity constraints.
- Large models are built from many connected patches.
- Patches are tessellated into triangles for rendering.

## Example
Example: A car body is modeled from multiple spline patches joined with smooth continuity.
